The Air Jordan 4 is one of the most technically demanding sneakers to replicate well. The combination of mesh netting panels, plastic wing eyelets, visible Air unit, and specific sole tooling means there are multiple places where quality can fall short. Here is the full checklist.

Sole Unit: The Most Important Check

The Jordan 4 sole has a distinctive shape — it should have a slight upward curve toward the toe when the shoe is placed flat. A sole that sits completely flat is a sign of incorrect tooling. The outsole pattern should be clearly defined with sharp edges between the different sections.

For the icy sole colorways (Military Black, Bred, White Cement), the translucency should be visible in natural light. Hold QC photos up to assess — the icy portions should show some depth and light transmission, not look opaque.

Wing Eyelets

The plastic wing eyelets on the midfoot are a common quality tell. On accurate batches they are the correct color, have smooth edges, and sit flush with the upper. On lower-quality batches they can be slightly rough-edged or a slightly off color (particularly on Military Black where the wings should be a specific dark tone).

Mesh Panels

The mesh netting panels on the toe box and heel should have a consistent, fine grid pattern. Looser or coarser mesh is a sign of the wrong material. The color should match the surrounding leather accurately.
